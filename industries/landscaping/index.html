Why generic marketing doesn't work for landscapers

A generalist agency markets all of it the same way and floods you with mow-and-go tire-kickers in July, when your crews are already booked out. We market the way your business actually makes money.

The landscaping calendar

We market your company ahead of the seasons

Landscaping demand swings hard with the seasons, and the companies that win get ahead of it instead of chasing it. Most owners start advertising in April, when they're already drowning, then go dark in winter, exactly when they should be selling next year's biggest installs. We flip that: here's how we work your market through all four seasons.

Winter · Dec–Feb

Sell next year's installs

On the ground

Work slows, crews thin out, and cash gets tight.

What we market

Design consults and big builds while competitors hibernate, so your spring book is full before spring starts.

Spring · Mar–Apr

Capture the rush

On the ground

Demand explodes and speed-to-lead decides who wins.

What we market

Fast lead capture and maintenance sign-ups, converting the winter design backlog into signed installs.

Summer · May–Aug

Book the next wave

On the ground

Crews are maxed and you're booked out for weeks.

What we market

Fall projects and next year's design work, while we capture every finished job for your portfolio and reviews.

Fall · Sep–Nov

Upsell & pre-sell

On the ground

Cleanups, planting, and prime outdoor-lighting season.

What we market

Lighting and cleanup upsells to your base, plus winter design consults so the cycle never stops.

Run a warmer market? The swings flatten in the South and Southwest. We tune the calendar to your region, not a generic template.

Questions, answered

Landscaping marketing questions, answered

How do landscaping companies get more leads online?

With a portfolio-led website, strong local SEO and Google Business Profile, high-intent Google and Local Service Ads, and a steady flow of reviews, all working as one system. The landscapers who win rank for service-plus-city searches like "paver patio [town]" and answer new leads within minutes.

When should a landscaping company start marketing for spring?

In winter. The owners who fill their spring book are the ones selling design and installs in December and January, while competitors go quiet. By the time the spring rush arrives, it's too late to start, you're just chasing the same demand as everyone else.

How much does landscaping marketing cost?

It depends on your market, your goals, and how aggressively you want to grow, so we don't put a number on it before we understand your business. What we can tell you up front: everything is built as one system, there's no per-channel upsell, and the strategy call is free.

Do you only work with landscaping companies?

We work exclusively with the green industry: lawn, landscape, hardscape, and outdoor contractors. That focus is the point: we already understand your seasons, your margins, and your customers, so you're never explaining your business to your marketing team.

Will this work in my market and for my size?

We market only the service areas you declare (no wasting budget on towns you don't want) and we have a path for both six-figure companies getting found for the first time and seven-figure companies scaling toward market dominance.

What results can a landscaping company expect?

We don't guarantee specific numbers (no honest agency can), but real clients have grown from $700K to $1.3M in a year and tripled their monthly calls. Most owners feel momentum in 60–90 days, with real compounding around four to six months.

Do I need a new website, or can you work with mine?

Often a new, portfolio-led site is the highest-leverage first move, because it's what turns ad clicks and searches into booked estimates. But not always. On the strategy call we'll tell you honestly whether yours is worth keeping.

How do you handle the slow season?

We market ahead of the seasons. Winter is for selling next year's big installs and booking design work; fall is for lighting and cleanup upsells. The goal is a calendar that never goes silent, and in warmer markets, we tune the timing to your region.
